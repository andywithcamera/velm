## Why a Commercial License Exists

A commercial license can be useful where a customer wants rights or protections that are not provided by the public `AGPL-3.0-only` license, for example:

- operating a modified version without the AGPL network-source disclosure obligation;
- embedding Velm in a closed-source or proprietary product;
- OEM, white-label, or redistribution rights outside the AGPL terms;
- private support, warranty, service-level, or indemnity terms; or
- negotiated rights for internal-only distribution or regulated environments.

## Typical Scope

Any commercial license, if offered, may cover some or all of the following:

- production use under negotiated commercial terms;
- use, modification, and distribution rights outside the AGPL;
- closed-source integrations, extensions, or deployments;

The exact scope, fees, term, and restrictions depend on the signed commercial agreement.
